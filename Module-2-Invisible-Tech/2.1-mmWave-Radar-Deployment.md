# 2.1 mmWave Radar Deployment

## Council Discussion

### Nexus (Hardware/IoT Architect)
"Listen, if we want true invisibility, mmWave is the only way. Unlike PIR or cameras, mmWave radar detects micro-movements—even breathing. We can deploy a grid of TI-IWR series sensors in the ceiling. This gives us high-precision occupancy data without a single lens pointing at the baby. It's stable, it's private by design, and it feeds directly into our 'Oscar Moments' engine without risking the privacy of the caregivers."

### Lumina (Child Psychologist/EJE)
"I appreciate the lack of cameras, Nexus, but we must ask: what does this 'invisible eye' do to the emotional field of the room? A child doesn't perceive the radar, but they perceive the *result* of it. If the environment reacts too surgically—lights changing the instant a baby shifts—it can feel uncanny, almost robotic. We need to ensure the tech supports the bond, not replaces the intuition of the caregiver. The radar should be a safety net, not a director."

### Sora (Experience Designer)
"Lumina is right. The 'feeling' must be organic. Nexus, your sensors must be completely recessed—no plastic domes, no blinking LEDs. I want the data to drive 'soft' transitions. Instead of a binary 'occupied/vacant' trigger, let's use the micro-movement data to create a 'presence glow'. As the baby moves toward a zone, the lighting doesn't just snap on; it breathes into existence. The tech should feel like a living extension of the architecture, not a surveillance grid."

### Atlas (Strategic Realist/Compliance)
"Let's bring this back to the ground. From a PMI (Preventing Maternal/Infant Injury) and regulatory standpoint, non-optical sensing is a massive win for privacy compliance. However, I'm looking at the BOM (Bill of Materials). A full grid of mmWave sensors is significantly more expensive than standard PIR. We need a hybrid approach: mmWave in high-criticality zones (sleep area, transition boundaries) and cheaper sensors elsewhere. Also, we need a fail-safe. If a sensor glitches, we cannot have a 'blind spot' in a nursery. I need a redundancy plan before I sign off on the budget."

## Final Consensus Conclusion

The Council agrees on the deployment of mmWave radar for its privacy-preserving and high-precision capabilities, with the following mandates:

1. **Hybrid Deployment Strategy**: To balance budget and precision, mmWave sensors will be prioritized for high-criticality zones (e.g., sleep areas, transition boundaries), while standard PIR sensors will cover low-risk zones.
2. **Organic Environmental Feedback**: All hardware must be completely recessed and invisible. The resulting data will not trigger binary switches but "soft" responses (gradual lighting shifts, ambient sound modulation) to prevent an "uncanny" or robotic atmosphere.
3. **Psychological Safeguard**: The system is positioned as a safety net to augment caregiver intuition, ensuring that the technology remains a background support rather than a foreground director of the child's experience.
4. **Operational Redundancy**: A low-resolution secondary sensing layer will be implemented to ensure no critical blind spots occur during primary sensor failure, meeting Atlas's requirement for operational stability and PMI compliance.
